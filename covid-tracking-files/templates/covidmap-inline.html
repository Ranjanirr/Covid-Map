COVID-19 in the U.S

This map displays cumulative case, cumulative death and 14-day average incidence rate data from the Covid Tracking Project; it also shows infection probability and contact budget based on a mathematical model* applied to COVID-19.

 

Cases: Total cases to date per million population

Deaths: Total deaths to date per million population

Incidence Rate: Average number of cases per day over the last 14 days per million population

Infection Probability*: The probability that one will be infected after 100 contacts

Contact Budget*: The maximum number of contacts to ensure that the infection probability is below 5%

 

Epidemiological parameter values* used by the model for computing infection probability and contact budget: Transmission probability per contact = 10%; Infection-Fatality Ratio (IFR) = 0.0064; Lag between infection and death = 24 days; Serial interval = 4.2 days

 

Sources of COVID-19 data used: The Covid Tracking Project, Rt Live

*See paper here. The paper is work-in-progress and has not been peer-reviewed. Please use information here at your own risk and follow public health guidelines.
