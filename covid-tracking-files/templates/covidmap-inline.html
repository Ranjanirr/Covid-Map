COVID-19 in the U.S

This map displays cumulative case, cumulative death and 14-day average incidence rate data from the Covid Tracking Project. It also shows infection probability and contact budget based on a mathematical model* applied to COVID-19.

 

Cases: Total cases to date per million population

Deaths: Total deaths to date per million population

Incidence Rate: Average number of cases per day over the last 14 days per million population

Infection Probability*: The probability that one will be infected after 100 contacts over the next few days

Contact Budget*: The maximum number of contacts over the next few days to ensure that the infection probability is below 5%

 

The model* for computing the infection probability and contact budget rely on a number of epidemiological parameter values published in peer-reviewed literature, COVID-19 data and some assumptions. Therefore, we the absolute numbers are likely to have some inaccuracy. However, the relative values between states should be more reliable (e.g. asking “Am I more likely to be infected if I go to Massachusetts vs Connecticut”?)

 

Sources of COVID-19 data used: The Covid Tracking Project, Rt Live

*See paper here. The paper has not been peer-reviewed. Please use information here at your own risk and follow public health guidelines.
